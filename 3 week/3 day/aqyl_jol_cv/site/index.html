The parent project

Aqyl Jol — an AI digital twin for the city

Aqyl Jol (Kazakh for “Smart Road”) is an AI-based digital twin for energy-efficient urban transport and municipal fleet coordination. It fuses computer vision (YOLOv8/v11), causal machine learning, a multilingual LLM assistant, and an iOS app — turning ordinary city cameras into a real-time understanding of traffic and road hazards.

Validated in Astana (−40°C to +40°C), it powers snowplow routing, congestion-aware navigation and fleet dispatch that cut fuel and emissions. This Day-3 build reproduces its perception layer — the part that turns pixels into “what's on the road”.

  • 0.58–0.70vehicle AP0.5
  • 60–125FPS detection
  • 0.69–0.85road-hazard mAP50
  • 78–85%congestion accuracy

02

This build

Rebuilding the vision layer, on a laptop

I rebuilt the perception layer with three convolutional networks, each matched to its data — all trained and run locally on Apple-Silicon (MPS), no cloud. Two detectors draw typed boxes; one classifier reads the weather. Together: what hazard is on the road, what the weather is, and what vehicles are moving.

Model 01 · Detection

Road quality

YOLOv8 · fine-tuned on 2,009 road images

Finds surface hazards and boxes each one with its type — pothole, crack, manhole. The road-side twin of the vehicle detector: it marks where and what the damage is.

0mAP50 ≈ 0.40 · ~12 epochs on MPS
Model 02 · Classification

Road condition

ResNet18 · transfer learning on DAWN

Reads the weather on the road — fog, rain, snow, sand. A frozen ImageNet backbone with a fresh head. Grad-CAM confirms it looks at road, sky and weather texture — not artifacts.

0validation accuracy · snow 0.93 · sand 0.87
Model 03 · Detection

Traffic in motion

YOLOv8 · pretrained (COCO), run on video

Detects and types vehicles as they move — car, bus, bicycle, truck — drawing a labelled box on each. Real-time on a laptop GPU, echoing Aqyl Jol's 60–125 FPS.
